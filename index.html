Scenario 3: Professional Communication and Reputation (Non-LLM Published Version)

You are an engineer working on a project that requires expertise from another region.

To find a suitable professional, you contact a representative from a regional engineering society, who recommends a qualified engineer.

You proceed to hire this recommended engineer.

During the project, you become dissatisfied with the engineer’s performance due to:

  • Lack of regular communication
  • Concerns about overall service quality

Instead of raising these concerns directly with the engineer, you:

  • Contact the society representative who made the recommendation
  • Express your dissatisfaction with the engineer
  • Indicate interest in hiring a different engineer

Later, the engineer you hired contacts you and expresses strong dissatisfaction, stating that your concerns were shared without giving them an opportunity to respond or improve.

What would you do in this situation?

Options:

A. Communicate concerns directly with the engineer before involving others
B. Report dissatisfaction to the recommending body without informing the engineer
C. Raise concerns with both the engineer and the recommending body simultaneously
D. Replace the engineer without providing detailed feedback
